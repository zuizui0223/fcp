# Current-data research results

## Journal-facing scope

The study concerns **documented intraspecific flower-colour variation** and distinguishes within-population coexistence from geographically structured variation. The dataset is a literature-derived comparative sample, not a random sample of angiosperms and not an estimate of worldwide prevalence.

## Evidence-base snapshot

The resolved acquisition dataset contains 664 candidate species, including 111 validated natural cases and 553 deferred candidates. Candidate validation is strongly associated with retained literature effort, so ascertainment remains explicit throughout interpretation.

## Candidate-versus-control climatic niches

The matched analysis included 70 focal species and 243 taxonomically matched controls. Across five occupied-climate metrics and four occurrence-cell thresholds, there was no clear evidence that documented colour-variable species generally occupied broader climatic niches than matched controls.

## Audited spatial-organization analysis

### Classification audit

The source audit preserved source title, DOI or OpenAlex identifier, evidence text and decision note for every retained strict-set species. A text-pattern audit identified that the classifier did not recognize plural `within populations`. The rule was corrected, and cases containing evidence for both within- and among-population structure are now assigned `mixed` rather than forced into the binary model.

`Lilium sulphureum` consequently left the strict binary set. `Lotus corniculatus` remained `among_population` because its retained evidence describes a major morph-frequency difference among Scottish regions without documenting local coexistence in the retained passage.

### Model specification

- response: `among`, coded 1 for `among_population` and 0 for `within_population`;
- formula: `among ~ metric_z + effort_z`;
- estimator: `statsmodels` 0.14.6 GLM, binomial family, logit link;
- covariance: family-clustered sandwich standard errors;
- confidence interval: Wald 95% CI from clustered uncertainty;
- complementary inference: 9,999 label permutations and leave-one-family-out refits.

Family clustering and family deletion do not constitute a phylogenetic comparative analysis.

> In the audited baseline-unambiguous set, geographically structured flower-colour variation showed a negative association with species-level realised moisture niche breadth. This is an exploratory focal association among several evaluated climatic metrics. It is not a preregistered confirmation, a causal result or a claim based solely on crossing a 0.05 threshold.

### Broader evidence-classified set

The broader set contained **51 species from 29 families**: 33 within-population and 18 geographically structured cases.

For moisture breadth, the estimate attenuated to OR 0.563, 95% CI 0.292–1.085, clustered Wald p 0.0861 and permutation p 0.0944. Leave-one-family-out ORs ranged from 0.445 to 0.616 and remained below one.

The attenuation is a central evidence-quality result and must be reported beside the strict estimate.

### Enrichment-only subset

The high-confidence enrichment-only subset contained 17 species from 11 families: 13 within-population and four geographically structured cases. It remained below the prespecified 20-species minimum and is reported as `not_estimable`.

## Multiplicity context

The pipeline exports a complete five-metric by four-threshold matrix. Moisture breadth is the focal reported association identified during comparative evaluation. Interpretation must combine effect size, interval width, permutation support, evidence-set attenuation and family-deletion stability rather than treating one p-value as the discovery criterion.

## Coarse sampled-distribution checks

The examined GBIF fragmentation and generic environmental-turnover metrics did not clearly account for the focal association. These are negative controls only. Non-significance does not prove absence, and unsupervised GBIF components are not biological populations, barriers or morph distributions.

## Taxonomic and mechanistic limits

The analysis does not establish climate causation, local adaptation, morph-specific climatic niches, gene-flow limitation, phylogenetic independence or a universal angiosperm rule. Testing mechanism requires colour-state-labelled localities or named populations linked to environmental conditions.

## Journal-facing conclusions

The evidence supports four bounded conclusions:

1. Documentation is strongly shaped by publication effort.
2. Documented colour-variable species do not generally occupy broader climatic niches than matched controls.
3. The audited strict set shows a negative moisture-breadth association with narrow clustered-interval support, borderline permutation support and stable family-deletion direction.
4. The estimate weakens when less certain classifications are included, making evidence quality part of the inference.

The central contribution is the explicit comparison of documented spatial organization while tracking ascertainment, classification uncertainty and inferential sensitivity.

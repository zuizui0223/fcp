# Current-data research results

## Journal-facing scope

The study concerns **documented intraspecific flower-colour variation** and distinguishes:

1. **within-population flower-colour polymorphism**, where discrete colour morphs coexist within at least one natural population; and
2. **geographically structured flower-colour variation**, where colour states are differentiated primarily among populations or regions without demonstrated local coexistence.

The dataset is a global literature-derived comparative sample of documented cases. It is not a random sample of angiosperms and does not estimate worldwide prevalence.

## Evidence-base snapshot

The resolved acquisition dataset contains 664 candidate species, including 111 validated cases of natural intraspecific flower-colour variation and 553 deferred candidates. Candidate validation is strongly associated with retained literature effort, so ascertainment must remain explicit in interpretation.

## Candidate-versus-control climatic niches

The matched analysis included 70 focal species and 243 taxonomically matched controls. Across five occupied-climate metrics and four occurrence-cell thresholds, there was no clear evidence that documented colour-variable species generally occupied broader climatic niches than matched controls. All evaluated specifications are exported rather than selecting only the focal association.

### Model specification

- response: `among`, coded 1 for `among_population` and 0 for `within_population`;
- formula: `among ~ metric_z + effort_z`;
- `metric_z`: standardized occupied-climate metric;
- `effort_z`: standardized `log1p(n_climate_cells)`;
- estimator: `statsmodels` 0.14.6 GLM with binomial family and logit link;
- covariance: family-clustered sandwich standard errors;
- confidence interval: Wald 95% CI from the family-clustered standard error;
- complementary inference: 9,999 label permutations and leave-one-family-out refits.

The phrase `family-adjusted` should not be used without this specification. Family clustering and family-deletion sensitivity do not constitute a phylogenetic comparative analysis.

### Broader evidence-classified set

The broader set contained **52 species from 30 families**: 33 within-population and 19 geographically structured cases.

For moisture breadth, the estimate attenuated to OR 0.566, 95% CI 0.293–1.093, clustered Wald p 0.0901 and permutation p 0.0886. The direction remained negative after every leave-one-family-out refit, with ORs from 0.447 to 0.622.

For PCA hull area, OR was 0.700, 95% CI 0.442–1.109, clustered Wald p 0.129 and permutation p 0.3111.

The attenuation in the broader set is a central evidence-quality result and must be reported beside the strict estimate.

### Enrichment-only subset

The high-confidence enrichment-only subset contained 18 species from 12 families: 14 within-population and 4 geographically structured cases. It remained below the prespecified 20-species minimum and is therefore reported as `not_estimable`, with zero inferential permutations.

## Multiplicity context

The pipeline exports the complete five-metric by four-threshold matrix. Moisture breadth should be described as the **focal reported association identified during comparative evaluation**, unless dated prospective documentation establishes otherwise. A single p-value below 0.05 is not the discovery criterion; effect size, interval width, permutation support, evidence-set attenuation and family-deletion stability must be interpreted together.

## Coarse sampled-distribution checks

The examined GBIF point-cloud fragmentation metrics did not account for the focal association after moisture breadth and sampling effort were included. Likewise, generic climatic turnover among unlabelled occurrence components did not show a clear association.

These analyses are negative controls only. Non-significance does not prove absence, and unsupervised GBIF components are not biological populations, dispersal barriers or morph distributions.

## Taxonomic and mechanistic limits

Family-clustered uncertainty and leave-one-family-out analyses reduce sensitivity to single-family dominance but do not demonstrate phylogenetic independence. Species-level realised occupied climate is not physiological tolerance, and the occurrence records are not labelled by flower-colour state.

The analysis therefore does not establish:

- climate causation;
- local adaptation;
- morph-specific climatic niches;
- gene-flow limitation; or
- a universal rule for angiosperms.

Testing those mechanisms requires colour-state-labelled localities or named populations linked to environmental conditions.

## Journal-facing conclusions

The current evidence supports four bounded conclusions:

1. Documentation of flower-colour variation is strongly shaped by publication effort.
2. Documented colour-variable species do not generally occupy broader climatic niches than matched controls.
3. In the frozen baseline-unambiguous set, geographically structured variation is associated with narrower species-level realised moisture niche breadth.
4. The association weakens when less certain classifications are included, making evidence quality part of the biological inference.

The central contribution is the explicit comparison of the documented spatial organization of intraspecific flower-colour variation while tracking ascertainment and classification uncertainty.
